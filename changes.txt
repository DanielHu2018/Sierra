## Sierra MVP – Required Fixes and Feature Improvements

### 1. Core UX / Functionality Issues

* **Simulation Flow**

  * Users cannot exit a simulation to start a new one. This must be fixed.
  * Add a clear “Exit Simulation” or “Reset Scenario” control that returns the app to its initial state.

* **Data Visibility**

  * The **Data Layers** and **Archive** tabs are empty or non-functional.
  * Ensure datasets are properly loaded, selectable, and visually rendered on the map.

---

### 2. Map & Interaction Improvements

* **Map Styling**

  * Switch to a **dark-themed map** while preserving:

    * Realistic geography
    * Terrain/topology detail
    * Global coverage
  * Avoid overly minimal styles—retain physical realism.

* **Pin Interaction Enhancements**

  * Source and destination pins must:

    * Use **distinct colors**
    * Provide **clear visual confirmation** when placed (animation, glow, or marker change)
  * Add:

    * A **cancel/undo pin placement** option
    * A clear “placement mode” indicator (so users know what they’re doing)

---

### 3. Routing Logic Improvements

* **Optimization Objective**

  * Routes should prioritize **maximizing population served**, not just distance or cost.
  * Introduce a **population-served metric**:

    * Display total number of people served directly on each route
    * Make this a primary comparison factor for recommendations

* **Multiple Route Strategies**

  * Generate and display multiple routes:

    * Least-cost route
    * Maximum population served
    * Renewable-optimized route

---

### 4. Risk Visualization

* After a simulation:

  * Risks must be **geographically visible on the map**
  * Each risk should:

    * Be clickable
    * Automatically navigate/zoom the user to its location
  * Include visual markers (heatspots, icons, overlays)

---

## 5. Strategic Upgrade: Princeton NZA + REPEAT Integration

### Goal

Transform Sierra from a routing tool into a **grid planning and infrastructure intelligence platform**.

---

### High-Value Data Layers to Integrate

* **Renewable Potential (Wind & Solar)**

  * Use Optimal Renewable Buildout (ORB) data
  * Identify Candidate Project Areas (CPAs)
  * Use as weighted nodes in routing

* **Offshore Wind (ERCOT-Relevant)**

  * Incorporate Gulf of Mexico potential from E+ scenarios
  * Critical for long-term grid reliability

* **Solar Resource Quality**

  * Use irradiance data as routing weights
  * Prefer high-yield zones

* **CO₂ Storage & Infrastructure**

  * Map:

    * Saline aquifers
    * Depleted oil/gas reservoirs (Permian Basin, Gulf Coast)
  * Flag proximity to thermal plants

* **Transmission Expansion Corridors**

  * Use projected GW-km transmission corridors
  * Treat as “preferred paths” for routing

---

### Integration Strategy (Agent-Based Reasoning)

* **Constraint Surface**

  * Use CLUA (Constrained Land Use Assumptions) to define:

    * No-go zones (protected land, ecological constraints)

* **Scenario-Based Routing**

  * Generate routes based on:

    * Cost optimization
    * Renewable connectivity (CPA-driven)
    * Environmental preservation

* **AI Justification Layer**

  * Each recommended route must include reasoning such as:

    * Alignment with projected transmission corridors
    * Connection to high-value renewable zones
    * Avoidance of constrained land areas

* **Impact Scoring**

  * Attach metrics to each route:

    * Population served
    * Jobs created
    * Emissions reduced
    * Public health impact

---

### 48-Hour MVP Focus (ERCOT Scope)

Prioritize:

* Texas-specific datasets
* High-resolution geospatial overlays
* Fast integration over completeness

Immediate assets to use:

* Texas state-level metrics (jobs, CO₂ storage, air quality)
* Transmission expansion maps (2030 / 2050)
* Updated REPEAT data aligned with current policy landscape

---

## Bottom Line

Fix the broken UX first. Then upgrade routing to be population- and infrastructure-aware. Finally, layer in Princeton and REPEAT data to make Sierra’s recommendations defensible, data-driven, and strategically meaningful.
