Building new transmission is one of the most complex infrastructure challenges of our time. Routes must thread through private land, sensitive ecosystems, regulatory boundaries, and existing grid corridors — while minimizing cost and maximizing energy delivery.

Sierra uses AI to simulate multiple possible paths simultaneously, scoring each against weighted criteria and generating a ranked recommendation that planners can defend to regulators and communities.

From site selection to impact assessment, Sierra compresses months of desktop study into a single session.

Data Intelligence

Grid ERCOT Grid Overlay Live integration with the Texas grid network, showing existing transmission corridors and substation interconnection points.
Environmental Wildlife Habitat Mapping Species habitat boundaries and migration corridors — flagging ecological sensitivity zones that trigger constraint analysis.
Legal Eminent Domain Risk Land parcel analysis that identifies where condemnation proceedings are likely, letting routes avoid high-friction ownership clusters.
Terrain Topography & Friction Elevation models and construction friction heatmaps that quantify the cost premium of routing across steep or unstable terrain.
Boundary Land Classification Federal, state, and protected land boundaries that define where transmission can be sited under current permitting regimes.
Alerts Environmental Triggers AI-generated alerts when a proposed route intersects known risk factors — floodplains, wetlands, critical habitat, or seismic zones.
